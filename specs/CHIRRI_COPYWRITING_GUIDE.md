# Chirri Copywriting Guide
## How to Write Landing Page Copy That Doesn't Smell Like AI

---

## 1. AI Slop Detector -- 20 Patterns to NEVER Use

These are the hallmarks of AI-generated marketing copy. If you see any of these in Chirri's copy, delete them immediately.

| # | Pattern | Example (BAD) | Why It's Obvious |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **"Unlock the power of..."** | "Unlock the power of automated invoicing" | No human says "unlock the power" in conversation |
| 2 | **"Seamlessly integrate..."** | "Seamlessly integrate with your existing tools" | "Seamlessly" is the #1 AI tell word |
| 3 | **"Take your X to the next level"** | "Take your business to the next level" | Empty escalation. What level? |
| 4 | **"You deserve better than..."** | "You deserve better than spreadsheets" | Patronizing. The reader knows what they deserve. |
| 5 | **"In today's fast-paced world..."** | "In today's fast-paced digital landscape..." | Filler that says nothing |
| 6 | **"Whether you're a... or a..."** | "Whether you're a freelancer or an enterprise" | The hedging two-audience opener |
| 7 | **"Say goodbye to X"** | "Say goodbye to manual data entry" | Infomercial energy |
| 8 | **"Introducing..."** | "Introducing the future of project management" | Self-important. Just show the thing. |
| 9 | **"Designed for [vague group]"** | "Designed for modern teams" | What's a non-modern team? |
| 10 | **"Effortlessly manage..."** | "Effortlessly manage your workflow" | Nothing is effortless. Devs know this. |
| 11 | **"Streamline your..."** | "Streamline your operations" | Corporate buzzword bingo |
| 12 | **"All-in-one solution"** | "Your all-in-one business solution" | Means nothing specific |
| 13 | **"Revolutionize the way you..."** | "Revolutionize the way you build software" | Hyperbole that triggers skepticism |
| 14 | **"Empower your team to..."** | "Empower your team to collaborate better" | Vague, patronizing, corporate |
| 15 | **"Leverage the power of..."** | "Leverage the power of AI" | Double buzzword combo |
| 16 | **The dramatic one-liner paragraph** | "Your time is valuable.\n\nYour tools should be too." | ChatGPT's favorite structure |
| 17 | **"Trusted by thousands of..."** | "Trusted by thousands of businesses worldwide" | Show the logos or give a number, don't claim vaguely |
| 18 | **"Built for scale"** | "Built for scale from day one" | Everyone says this. What does it mean concretely? |
| 19 | **"Don't just X -- Y"** | "Don't just manage invoices -- master them" | The false upgrade framing |
| 20 | **Em dashes (—) everywhere** | "Your API changed — here's what to do" | The #1 AI punctuation tell. Use double hyphens (--) or rewrite the sentence. |
| 21 | **Excessive conjunctive phrases** | "Moreover, furthermore, additionally, in addition" | Filler connectors. Just start the next sentence. |
| 22 | **"Delve into", "Navigate", "Landscape"** | "Navigate the complex landscape of API changes" | AI vocabulary words that no developer has ever said unironically |
| 20 | **"It's time to..."** | "It's time to rethink how you handle payments" | False urgency. No it isn't. The reader will decide when. |

### The Meta-Pattern: AI Slop Structure

The worst AI copy follows this skeleton:
```
[Dramatic problem statement about the reader]
[You deserve better]
[Introducing our solution]
[Vague superlative about the solution]
[List of buzzword features]
[Call to action with false urgency]
```

Real companies don't do this. They state what the product is and show it working.

## 2. Ten Rules for Chirri's Copy Voice

### Rule 1: State what it does. Stop.
Linear's title tag: "Linear – The system for product development." Not "The revolutionary platform that transforms how teams build products." Just what it is.

**Chirri version:** "Chirri -- [what it actually does, in under 8 words]"

### Rule 2: Be specific, not superlative
Stripe doesn't say "we process a lot of payments." They say "$1.9T in payments volume processed in 2025." Neon says "150,000+ new Postgres compute endpoints provisioned daily." Numbers are immune to sounding like AI.

**Apply to Chirri:** Use real numbers. Response times, lines of code saved, actual metrics.

### Rule 3: Use the fewest words possible
Raycast's title tag: "Your shortcut to everything." Five words. Resend's hero: "Email for developers." Three words. The best SaaS heroes are under 6 words.

**Chirri target:** Hero headline should be 3-6 words.

### Rule 4: Show the product, don't describe it
Linear shows an actual board with real-looking issues. Stripe shows code snippets. Vercel shows deploy logs. The product IS the marketing.

**For Chirri:** Screenshots, code examples, or interactive demos > paragraphs explaining features.

### Rule 5: Write like you talk, not like you present
Fly.io: "Build fast. Run any code fearlessly." -- conversational, punchy. Railway: "Deploy anything without the complexity" -- direct, no filler.

**The test:** Read it aloud. If it sounds like a keynote slide, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd say to a coworker, keep it.

### Rule 6: Be opinionated
Linear is shaped by "the practices and principles of world-class product teams" -- they're saying: we know how this should work, and we built it that way. Fly.io: "The platform for devs who just want to ship" -- implicit opinion that other platforms get in the way.

**Chirri should have opinions** about how developer tools should work. State them plainly.

### Rule 7: Let features speak for themselves
Don't editorialize features. Compare:
- BAD: "Our powerful real-time sync engine ensures your data is always up-to-date across all devices"
- GOOD: "Real-time sync across devices" (Raycast-style)
- BETTER: Show it syncing in a demo

### Rule 8: Social proof with specificity
Stripe: logos + "$1.9T processed" + named customer quotes with titles. Clerk: "Trusted by fast-growing companies" + actual logos. Neon: "A Databricks company."

**For Chirri:** Real logos, real numbers, real quotes with names. Never "trusted by thousands."

### Rule 9: Technical accuracy over marketing polish
Developers will bounce the second they detect bullshit. Supabase says "The Postgres Development Platform" -- it's accurate. Neon says "Serverless Postgres" -- technically precise. Resend says "Email API for developers" -- exactly what it is.

**Chirri must be technically accurate.** If it uses a term, it should be the correct term.

### Rule 10: Confidence through understatement
The best companies don't try to convince you. They present the product and assume you're smart enough to see the value. Stripe's hero: "Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue." It's not begging. It's not hyping. It's just... what they are.

## 3. Competitor Study -- What Each Company Does Right

### Linear (linear.app)
- **Hero:** "The product development system for teams and agents"
- **Tagline (title):** "Linear – The system for product development"
- **Tone:** Precise, calm, confident. Almost austere.
- **What works:**
  - Section headers are verbs: "Make product operations self-driving," "Define the product direction," "Move work forward"
  - Minimal adjectives. They describe functions, not feelings.
  - The copy reads like documentation with good design, not marketing.
  - "A new species of product tool" -- one bold metaphor, used sparingly
- **Buzzwords:** Almost none. "Purpose-built" is the closest, and it's earned.
- **Text density:** Low. Lots of whitespace, the product UI does the talking.
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** It's too restrained. AI copy oversells. Linear undersells.

### Vercel (vercel.com)
- **Hero:** "Build and deploy the best web experiences with the AI Cloud"
- **One-liner:** "Vercel provides the developer tools and cloud infrastructure to build, scale, and secure a faster, more personalized web."
- **Tone:** Technical, confident, forward-leaning.
- **What works:**
  - "Your product, delivered." -- three words, says everything
  - Section titles are product categories, not hype: "Agents," "AI Apps," "Web Apps," "Composable Commerce"
  - "Framework-Defined Infrastructure" -- invented a term that's technically meaningful
  - "From code to infrastructure in one git push" -- specific, visual, accurate
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** Technical specificity. "Git push" is not a word AI marketing would use.

### Stripe (stripe.com)
- **Hero:** "Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue"
- **Sub:** "Accept payments, offer financial services, and implement custom revenue models—from your first transaction to your billionth."
- **Tone:** Authoritative, precise, institutional.
- **What works:**
  - Numbers everywhere: "135+ currencies," "$1.9T," "99.999% uptime," "200M+ active subscriptions"
  - Customer stories with specific metrics: "5+ consumer brands," "700+ store locations"
  - The book recommendation at the bottom (How Buildings Learn) -- pure personality
  - "Don't code? Set up billing, take in-person payments, or share a payment link" -- addresses a real use case simply
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** The density of real numbers and the literary book recommendation are deeply human touches.

### Raycast (raycast.com)
- **Hero:** "Your shortcut to everything"
- **Sub:** "A collection of powerful productivity tools all within an extendable launcher. Fast, ergonomic and reliable."
- **Secondary hero:** "It's not about saving time. It's about feeling like you're never wasting it."
- **Tone:** Clean, warm, slightly philosophical.
- **What works:**
  - Feature descriptions are one-liners: "Fast. Think in milliseconds." / "Native. Pure performance." / "Reliable. 99.8% crash-free rate."
  - "There's an extension for that" -- plays on a known phrase, not forced
  - "Take the short way." -- CTA that matches the product metaphor
  - The keyboard visual is the hero, not text
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** The copy has rhythm. Short. Punchy. Then longer. AI writes in monotone.

### Supabase (supabase.com)
- **Title:** "Supabase | The Postgres Development Platform"
- **Hero:** "Build in a weekend, scale to millions"
- **Tone:** Community-driven, open-source proud, understated.
- **What works:**
  - "Build in a weekend, scale to millions" -- concrete timeframe + concrete ambition
  - "Open source from day one" -- states a fact, not a marketing angle
  - "You're never locked in, and always in control" -- addresses a real developer fear simply
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** It sounds like a developer talking to developers. No performance.

### Resend (resend.com)
- **Hero:** "Email for developers"
- **Sub:** "The best way to reach humans instead of spam folders."
- **Tone:** Minimal, developer-native, slightly cheeky.
- **What works:**
  - "Email for developers" -- three words, instantly clear
  - "reach humans instead of spam folders" -- names the real problem without dramatics
  - "We are a team of engineers who love building tools for other engineers" -- direct, authentic
  - "The email platform we've always wished we had -- one that just works" -- relatable
  - "Email reimagined. Available today." -- understated closer
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** The specificity of the problem (spam folders) and the casual self-identification as engineers.

### Clerk (clerk.com)
- **Hero:** "Everything you need for authentication"
- **Sub:** "Ever feel like authentication requirements change with the season? Clerk keeps up."
- **Tone:** Practical, slightly conversational, developer-friendly.
- **What works:**
  - "Ever feel like authentication requirements change with the season?" -- relatable observation
  - Feature sections are use-case labeled: "B2B Authentication," "Frameworks," "Integrations"
  - "The easy solution to multi-tenancy" -- admits the problem is hard, positions as easy
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** The "change with the season" metaphor is specific and slightly quirky.

### Neon (neon.tech → neon.com)
- **Hero:** "Fast Postgres Databases for Teams and Agents"
- **Title:** "Neon Serverless Postgres -- Ship faster"
- **Tone:** Technical, bold, backed-by-credibility.
- **What works:**
  - "Ship faster" -- two words, one promise
  - Leading with "A DATABRICKS COMPANY" -- instant credibility signal
  - "Founded by Postgres committers" -- expertise claim that's verifiable
  - "150,000+ new Postgres compute endpoints provisioned daily" -- specific, impressive
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** Postgres committers don't need AI to write copy. The credibility is inherent.

### Railway (railway.com)
- **Hero:** "Deploy anything without the complexity"
- **Tone:** Friendly, clear, anti-complexity.
- **What works:**
  - "Connect your repo, Railway handles the rest" -- two-step value prop
  - "Correct config. All the time." -- fragment that's assertive
  - "Instant networking. Zero setup." -- numbers (zero/instant) doing the work
  - "Finally, a development workflow that actually flows" -- self-aware wordplay
  - "46.4M+ deploys per month (and counting)" -- oddly specific number = believable
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** The specificity of "46.4M" (not "millions of"). Real data is never round.

### Fly.io (fly.io)
- **Hero:** "Build fast. Run any code fearlessly."
- **Sub:** "The platform for devs who just want to ship."
- **Tone:** Casual, opinionated, personality-forward.
- **What works:**
  - "devs who just want to ship" -- identifies tribe, implies opinion about complexity
  - "Sandboxes That Feel Like a Superpower" -- allowed to be enthusiastic because it's specific
  - "Fork Off VMs Like They're Processes" -- technically accurate AND funny
  - "Get Right in Your Users' Faces" -- irreverent section header
  - "Check Em Out!" -- CTA that sounds like a person
  - Feature bullets: "KVM hardware-isolated, built on a memory-safe stack" -- deeply technical
- **Why it doesn't sound like AI:** It has a voice. It's playful AND technical. AI can't hold both.

## 5. Tone Guide -- How Chirri Should Sound

### The Voice: Linear meets Fly.io, filtered through kanso (簡素)

**Kanso** (簡素) -- simplicity, elimination of clutter. This is Chirri's core aesthetic principle applied to words.

### Chirri's voice is:

| Chirri IS | Chirri IS NOT |
|-----------|---------------|
| Specific | Vague |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Brief | Curt |
| Technical when needed | Jargon-heavy for show |
| Slightly opinionated | Preachy |
| Calm | Excited |
| Developer-native | Developer-pandering |
| Honest about limitations | Defensive |
| Dry humor (when earned) | Trying to be funny |

### Voice Examples

**How Chirri would describe a feature:**
> "Recurring invoices. Set once, sends monthly."

**How Chirri would NOT describe a feature:**
> "Automate your billing workflow with our powerful recurring invoice engine that seamlessly handles monthly charges so you can focus on what matters most -- building great software."

**How Chirri would address a pain point:**
> "Invoices shouldn't take longer than the work."

**How Chirri would NOT address a pain point:**
> "Tired of spending hours on invoicing? You're not alone. Thousands of developers just like you are losing precious time every month on tedious financial tasks."

**How Chirri would do social proof:**
> "[Logo] [Logo] [Logo] [Logo]" + "3,200 teams. $48M invoiced last month."

**How Chirri would NOT do social proof:**
> "Join thousands of satisfied customers who have transformed their business operations with Chirri."

### Influence Map

```
                    TECHNICAL
                       |
            Neon ---- Linear ---- Stripe
                       |
          UNDERSTATED --+-- BOLD
                       |
          Supabase --- Chirri --- Fly.io
                       |

                       |
                    CASUAL
```

Chirri sits in the center-left: **technical + slightly understated + developer-casual.** Not as institutional as Stripe, not as playful as Fly.io. Closest to Linear's restraint with Resend's developer empathy.

### The Japanese Principle

The copy should embody:
- **Kanso (簡素)** -- Simplicity. Remove what's unnecessary.
- **Ma (間)** -- Negative space. Let the whitespace do work. Not every section needs a paragraph.
- **Wabi (侘)** -- Quiet confidence. Beauty in understatement, not decoration.

This doesn't mean using Japanese words in the copy (that would be cringe). It means the *feeling* of the copy should be minimal, considered, and intentional.

## 6. The "Read It Aloud" Test

Before publishing any copy, read it aloud. Here's the checklist:

### ❌ It fails if:
- It sounds like a press release
- You wouldn't say it to a developer sitting next to you
- It has words you'd never use in conversation ("leverage," "utilize," "streamline," "empower")
- It takes more than one breath to read a sentence
- You feel embarrassed reading it aloud
- It could be the copy for ANY product (not specifically Chirri)
- A developer would roll their eyes hearing it

### ✅ It passes if:
- It sounds like something a smart, slightly tired developer would write
- You can picture the exact person nodding as they read it
- It says one specific thing clearly
- It has personality but isn't performing
- You could tweet it without it sounding like an ad
- A technical person would read it and think "yeah, exactly"

### Rewrite Exercise

Take any piece of Chirri copy and apply these transformations:

1. **Cut it in half.** Remove every word that isn't earning its place.
2. **Remove all adjectives.** Add back only the ones that are specific (not "powerful" but maybe "sub-100ms").
3. **Replace "we" with "it".** Instead of "we help you track expenses," try "tracks expenses."
4. **Add a number.** Replace any vague claim with a specific one.
5. **The friend test.** Would you text this to a friend to explain what Chirri does? If not, rewrite until you would.

### Before/After Examples

**Before:**
> "Chirri is a comprehensive financial management platform designed to empower developers and small teams with the tools they need to streamline their business operations and focus on what truly matters."

**After:**
> "Chirri tracks your money so you can write code."

**Before:**
> "Our seamless integration with popular developer tools ensures that your financial data is always synchronized across all platforms, providing you with real-time insights into your business health."

**After:**
> "Connects to GitHub, Stripe, and your bank. Everything in one dashboard."

**Before:**
> "Join the growing community of forward-thinking developers who have chosen Chirri as their trusted financial companion on the journey to building successful software businesses."

**After:**
> "3,200 developers use Chirri. Here's why →"
